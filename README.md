# Sports & Fitness Participation Analysis🏅

## Overview🗃️
This project analyzes survey data from 100 participants to understand patterns in sports and fitness participation across different demographic groups. The analysis focuses on identifying relationships between variables such as age group, gender, and sports participation using statistical techniques.

The objective of this study is to uncover meaningful insights about fitness engagement and participation trends through statistical analysis and data visualization.

---

## Dataset📂
- Source: Survey-based dataset
- Number of Participants: 100
- Variables Included:
  - Age Group
  - Gender
  - Sports Participation
  - Weekly Activity Frequency
  - Preferred Sports Type

The dataset was cleaned and structured before performing analysis.

---

## Objectives🧠
- Analyze demographic patterns in sports participation
- Examine relationships between age, gender, and fitness activity
- Apply statistical tests to determine significant associations
- Visualize participation trends through charts and graphs

## Key Insights📌

### 1. Age-Based Fitness Patterns
- The **19–25 age group** showed the highest daily participation in physical activity, but also had the highest number of individuals who **never exercise**, indicating mixed fitness habits among young adults.
- **Running** was the most common physical activity across all age groups.
- Participation in sports **declines with increasing age**, especially in the **46–65 age group**.

### 2. Gender Differences in Physical Activity
- **Males were slightly more active overall**, particularly in daily exercise and sports participation.
- **Females showed a stronger preference for yoga** and wellness-oriented activities.
- Statistical testing (Chi-square test) indicated a **significant association between gender and frequency of physical activity** (p < 0.05).

### 3. Residence-Based Activity Patterns
- Individuals living in **rural areas reported higher daily physical activity** compared to urban residents.
- Urban participants showed **higher levels of inactivity**, possibly due to sedentary lifestyle patterns.

### 4. Education and Fitness Engagement
- **Undergraduate and intermediate-level individuals** reported higher participation in regular physical activity.
- High school and PhD groups showed comparatively **lower engagement levels**.

### 5. Popular Fitness Goals
- **Weight loss and muscle gain** were the most common fitness goals across participants.
- Statistical testing showed **no significant association between age group and fitness goals** (p > 0.05).

### 6. Technology and Fitness Tracking
- **Smartwatches and mobile apps** were the most commonly used fitness tracking tools.
- Although females showed slightly higher usage rates, **no significant relationship was found between gender and the type of fitness tracking device used**.

### 7. Statistical Test Findings
Chi-square tests revealed the following relationships:

**Significant Associations**
- Gender and frequency of physical activity
- Age group and participation in sports

**No Significant Associations**
- Age group and fitness goals
- Gender and type of fitness tracking device used

## Conclusion🧠

This project analyzed sports and fitness participation patterns using survey data collected from **100 respondents**. The study explored how demographic factors such as **age, gender, residence, and education** influence physical activity behavior.

The results indicate that **age and gender play an important role in shaping fitness participation patterns**. Younger individuals tend to participate more actively in sports, while participation gradually decreases with increasing age. Gender differences were also observed in the type and frequency of physical activity.

The study also highlights that **running and yoga are among the most popular forms of exercise**, while activities like gymnastics show relatively low participation levels. Rural residents were found to be more physically active than urban residents, likely due to lifestyle differences.

Statistical analysis using **Chi-square tests** helped validate relationships between key variables and provided evidence-based insights into fitness behavior.

Overall, the findings demonstrate how **statistical analysis and survey data can be used to understand health and fitness trends**, which may help researchers, policymakers, and fitness organizations design better health promotion strategies.
